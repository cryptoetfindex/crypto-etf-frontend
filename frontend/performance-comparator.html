Guide to Reading the Charts

Each chart type offers a different perspective on ETF performance. Use the one that best fits your analysis style.

Bar Chart

Classic vertical bars — ideal for comparing absolute returns across ETFs. The longest bar indicates the highest return. Great for quick rankings.

Horizontal Bar Chart

Same as bar chart but sideways — better when you have many ETFs or long labels, as horizontal layout prevents overlapping text.

Line Chart

Connects data points with lines — emphasizes relative performance and ranking. Useful for spotting consistent leaders or laggards.

Radar Chart

Displays values on axes radiating from center — best with 3–8 ETFs. Shows overall "shape" of performance (e.g., balanced vs. extreme).

Doughnut / Pie Chart

Shows proportion of total return — highlights market share of performance. Note: only meaningful with positive returns (negative values not shown well).

Polar Area Chart

Similar to pie but area reflects magnitude — emphasizes largest performers visually. Good for seeing dominance at a glance.

Tip: Switch between time periods (YTD, 1Y, Since Inception) to see how rankings change over different horizons.
