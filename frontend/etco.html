Grayscale Ethereum Covered Call ETF (ETCO)

Detailed overview of the Grayscale Ethereum Covered Call ETF (ETCO), an Ethereum-focused fund using a covered call strategy to generate income. Data as of January 01, 2026.

ETCO Overview

The Grayscale Ethereum Covered Call ETF (ETCO) seeks to provide investors with exposure to Ethereum (ETH) while generating additional income through a covered call options strategy. By writing call options on its ETH holdings, ETCO aims to capture option premiums, which can enhance returns in flat or declining markets but may cap upside potential in strong bull markets.

Key Features

  • Income Generation: Utilizes covered call options to generate premium income, ideal for investors seeking yield in addition to ETH exposure.
  • Ethereum Exposure: Provides direct exposure to Ethereum price movements, tempered by the covered call strategy.
  • Grayscale Expertise: Managed by Grayscale, a leader in crypto investment products with a strong track record.
  • Liquidity: Trades on major U.S. exchanges, offering easy access for investors.
  • Risk Management: The covered call strategy can reduce downside risk in volatile markets but may limit gains in sharp rallies.

Performance Insights

Performance data for ETCO is limited as of January 2026 due to its recent launch. The covered call strategy typically results in lower volatility compared to spot Ethereum ETFs (e.g., ETHA, FETH), but it may underperform in strong bullish markets due to capped upside. Investors should monitor option premium yields and Ethereum price trends to assess ETCO’s effectiveness.

Risks & Considerations

  • Market Risk: Ethereum price volatility can impact returns, even with the covered call strategy.
  • Options Risk: Covered calls limit upside potential in strong bull markets, and option premiums may vary.
  • Fees: Variable expense ratio may affect long-term returns; verify current fees with Grayscale.
  • Liquidity Risk: Lower AUM and trading volume may result in wider bid-ask spreads.
  • Regulatory Risk: Changes in crypto regulations could affect ETCO’s operations or Ethereum’s market.
