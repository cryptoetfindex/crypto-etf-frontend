What This Historical Bubble Map Shows

This visualization captures the state of the crypto ETF market as of January 3, 2026, based on comprehensive historical data.

  • Bubble Size = Assets Under Management (AUM): The largest bubble is IBIT (iShares Bitcoin Trust) at $67.91 billion — the clear market leader. Bitcoin spot ETFs dominate with over $115 billion combined AUM, showing massive institutional adoption of direct Bitcoin exposure.
  • X-Axis = Volatility: Bitcoin ETFs spread across high volatility (50-78%), reflecting crypto's inherent price swings. Ethereum ETFs cluster lower (20-60%), while many multi-asset and staking products show near-zero reported volatility (likely due to structured or covered call strategies).
  • Y-Axis = Expense Ratio: Most spot ETFs charge very low fees (0-0.3%), making them attractive. Leveraged and complex products (e.g., 2x ETFs) have higher fees (up to 2.67%). Grayscale's legacy trusts (GBTC, ETHE) remain expensive at 1.5-2.5%.
  • Colors: Bitcoin (orange) completely dominates the landscape. Ethereum (purple) forms a solid secondary cluster. Emerging Solana funds (green) and diversified/multi-asset ETFs (blue) are smaller but growing.

Key Historical Insights: Bitcoin spot ETFs attracted massive inflows ($1.5B+ estimated), while legacy trusts like GBTC saw outflows due to high fees. Ethereum ETFs gained traction with staking yields. The market shows maturation — low-cost spot products winning market share, while innovative leveraged/covered call strategies carve niches.
