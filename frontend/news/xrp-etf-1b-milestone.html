XRP ETFs Surpass $1 Billion AUM Milestone with Zero Outflow Days

Emerging altcoin ETFs demonstrate remarkable investor demand and resilience amid broader market volatility.

Spot XRP ETFs have officially crossed the $1 billion mark in combined assets under management (AUM), achieving this milestone just weeks after their mid-November 2025 launch.

According to data from multiple issuers and flow trackers, the category has recorded consistent daily inflows and zero outflow days since inception — a performance that stands in stark contrast to the mixed flows seen in Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs during the same period.

Key Highlights

  • • Combined AUM now exceeds $1.2 billion across major funds (Canary Capital, Grayscale, Bitwise, 21Shares, Franklin Templeton, etc.).
  • • Daily inflows averaging $20-50 million, with several record days above $100 million.
  • • No single day of net outflows recorded — the only major crypto ETF category to achieve this in late 2025.
  • • Outperforming Bitcoin ETFs (which saw weekly outflows of $582M) and Ethereum ETFs (mixed results).

Why the Strong Demand?

Analysts attribute the surge to:

  • • Resolution of long-standing regulatory uncertainty around XRP's security status.
  • • Growing institutional interest in diversified crypto exposure beyond BTC and ETH.
  • • Competitive fee structures (ranging from 0.35% to 1.15%).
  • • Positive market sentiment toward "altcoin season" narratives in Q4 2025.

Outlook

The XRP ETF category's performance has solidified its position as the fastest-growing segment among newly launched spot crypto products. Industry observers suggest this momentum could continue into 2026 if regulatory tailwinds persist and broader crypto adoption accelerates.
