How to Buy Crypto ETFs in 2025

Step-by-step beginner guide to purchasing spot crypto ETFs (Bitcoin, Ethereum, XRP, etc.) through traditional brokerage accounts.

  1. Choose a Brokerage Account

    Most major brokers now support crypto ETFs. Popular options include:

    • Fidelity – Commission-free on many funds, excellent for FBTC/FETH.
    • Charles Schwab – Low costs, good research tools.
    • Vanguard – Great for long-term investors (supports major ETFs).
    • Robinhood – Simple app, commission-free, popular with younger investors.
    • Interactive Brokers or eToro – Advanced features and international access.

    If you don’t have an account, sign up (requires ID verification). Fund it via bank transfer.

  2. Search for the ETF Ticker

    Use the search/barter function in your broker app or website. Common tickers (as of December 2025):

    • Bitcoin: IBIT, FBTC, GBTC, ARKB, BITB
    • Ethereum: ETHA, FETH, ETHE
    • Emerging: Various XRP/Solana tickers (check directory)

    Review the ETF details page on the broker for fees, holdings, and performance.

  3. Place Your Order

    Select “Buy”, enter dollar amount or number of shares.

    • Use market order for immediate execution (during trading hours: 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET).
    • Or limit order to buy at a specific price.
    • Most brokers have $0 commissions on ETFs.

    Confirm and submit. Shares appear in your account usually same-day or T+1.

  4. Consider Account Type

    For tax advantages:

    • IRA/401(k): Tax-deferred growth (many brokers offer crypto ETFs in retirement accounts).
    • Taxable brokerage: More flexibility but capital gains apply.
  5. Best Practices & Tips

    • Start small and dollar-cost average (DCA).

    • Diversify across Bitcoin, Ethereum, or multi-asset funds.

    • Monitor regularly but avoid frequent trading due to volatility.

    • Keep records for taxes (brokers provide 1099 forms).
