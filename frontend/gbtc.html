Recent Performance & Flows

  • • Year-to-date: Significant outflows post-conversion, but remains liquid and widely held.
  • • Tracks spot Bitcoin price; historically traded at premium/discount before ETF conversion.

Data approximate; sources include Grayscale reports, ETF.com, Farside Investors, and market trackers (as of mid-December 2025).

Pros

  • • Long track record (since 2013) and established brand in crypto.
  • • High liquidity and availability on most brokers.
  • • Familiar to long-term Bitcoin investors from pre-ETF days.
  • • Large holdings provide market influence.

Cons

  • • Highest expense ratio at 1.50% – significantly more than competitors (0.20-0.25%).
  • • Ongoing outflows as investors seek lower-cost options.
  • • No competitive edge on fees or unique features.
  • • Same Bitcoin volatility risk as peers.

How to Buy GBTC

Available on nearly all major brokers including Fidelity, Vanguard, Charles Schwab, Robinhood, and interactive brokers. Search ticker "GBTC". Often held by legacy investors.
